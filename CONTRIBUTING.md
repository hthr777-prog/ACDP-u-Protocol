## 1. Before You Start

Before creating an Issue or submitting a Pull Request:

* Check whether a similar issue, discussion, or PR already exists.  
* Make sure your proposal aligns with the principles defined in the protocol‚Äôs `README.md`.

---

## 2. Content Rules (The Essence Criterion)

The central guiding principle of ACDP is strict **ontological neutrality**.

Please follow these content rules:

### 2.1 What *Not* to Do
* **Do not** create essences based on specific thinkers, schools, interpretations, or methodologies.  
  Classification must be based **only** on the inherent nature of the concept itself.
* **Do not** add political, social, or cultural examples unless they serve a clear and essential analytical purpose.
* **Do not** violate **Rule 1.5 (Consensus Protection)** by artificially equalizing concepts where an overwhelming expert consensus already exists.

### 2.2 What *Is* Acceptable
* Clarifications that reduce ambiguity.
* Improvements that enhance logical coherence.
* Additions that refine ontological distinctions according to the Essence Criterion.

---

## 3. Style Consistency

To maintain a uniform structure across the protocol:

* Use the same numbering pattern (1 ‚Üí 1.1 ‚Üí 1.1.2).  
* Use neutral, depersonalized, and ontologically precise language.  
* Avoid rhetorical or opinion-based phrasing.  
* Keep explanations concise and free from unnecessary examples.

---

## 4. Submission Process (GitHub Workflow)

All proposed changes must be submitted via a **Pull Request (PR)**.

### 4.1 Steps

1. **Fork the repository** to your GitHub account.  
2. **Create a new branch** with a descriptive name, e.g.,  
   `clarify/causality-section` or `fix/essence-definition`.  
3. **Make your changes** in that branch.  
4. **Write a clear commit message** summarizing *what* was changed and *why*.  
5. **Open a Pull Request**, including the required explanation (below).

---

## 5. Pull Request Requirements

Every PR must include:

1. **The Problem**  
   What ambiguity, inconsistency, or ontological issue are you addressing?

2. **The Solution**  
   A clear explanation of the change and how it aligns with the Essence Criterion.

3. **Testing**  
   If applicable, note whether your modification was checked against known ambiguity scenarios or protocol edge cases.

---

## 6. Review and Approval

* All PRs are reviewed by core maintainers.  
* Be prepared to discuss the ontological distinctions of any new essence introduced.  
* Once approved, the PR will be merged and tagged with the corresponding version in `VERSION_HISTORY.md`.

---

## 7. Code of Conduct

By contributing, you agree to maintain respectful and professional communication at all times.  
Disagreements should focus on the logic and ontology, not the individual contributors.

---

Thank you for helping maintain the integrity and clarity of the ACDP Protocol!
